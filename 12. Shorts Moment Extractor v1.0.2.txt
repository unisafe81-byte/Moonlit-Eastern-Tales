# SHORTS MOMENT DEFINITION

A Shorts moment is not a full plot beat.

It is not the whole story.

It is not the full explanation.

It is not the emotional resolution.

It is not the final moral meaning.

It is not a summary of the episode.

It is a **single quiet image or sensory sequence** that creates gentle curiosity.

A Shorts moment should be built around one of the following:

## 1. Unusual but non-threatening object

Examples:

* a dry letter beneath wet eaves
* a shell resting above the tide line
* a folded paper near low window light
* a brush beside an inkstone
* a small lantern still glowing
* a blue light on a damp stone
* a ferry rope beside wet planks
* a bowl of rainwater by a door
* a cloth sign moving above a closed market road

## 2. Trace without explanation

Examples:

* no footprints, but an object remains
* no visible visitor, but a letter rests there
* no spoken message, but a small mark remains
* no one returns, but the light stays low
* no clear source, but the sound continues
* no hand appears, but the rope is tied
* no boat moves, but water marks remain on the planks

## 3. Threshold between two spaces

Examples:

* wet courtyard and dry maru
* forest path and old house
* shore and tide line
* mountain path and clearing
* paper window and dark room
* old gate and moonlit road
* riverbank and ferry boat
* closed market and snowy road
* temple steps and low mist

## 4. Quiet sensory contrast

Examples:

* rain outside / dry wood inside
* dark water / pale shell
* blue glow / gray mist
* warm paper window / cool pine air
* low lantern light / wet stone
* soft tide / dry rock
* wet planks / pale moon reflection
* cold snow / low window light
* dark reeds / slow river sound

## 5. Soft mystery that remains unresolved

Examples:

* who left the letter is not explained
* why the shell remains is not explained
* where the blue light comes from is not explained
* why the lantern still burns is not explained
* why the object is placed there is not explained
* why the ferry rope is tied is not explained
* why the bell sound continues is not explained

The best Shorts moment should make the viewer think:

**“Why is this image so quiet, and why does it stay with me?”**

not:

**“What shocking thing happens next?”**

# MOMENT TYPE CLASSIFICATION

Each Shorts candidate must include one primary Moment Type.

Choose one of the following:

* **Object Mystery** — a quiet object remains without explanation
* **Hidden Trace** — a mark, ink, footprint, fold, sound, knot, waterline, reflection, or small sign is present but not explained
* **Spatial Threshold** — two spaces meet, such as rain/dry wood, shore/tide, path/house, forest/clearing, riverbank/boat, market/road
* **Light-Texture Close-Up** — light reveals paper, wood, stone, water, mist, shell, ink, fabric, rope, reeds, snow, or other material texture
* **Sound Motif** — a repeated sound creates atmosphere, such as rain, tide, bell, bamboo, pine wind, water drops, river current, market cloth, snow-softened footsteps
* **Fade-Out Atmosphere** — a near-ending image where fewer details remain
* **Soft Folklore Trace** — a folklore-related image appears indirectly, such as pale blue light, mist, shell, mountain presence, quiet animal trace, river light, or an unexplained but harmless sign

Do not choose a Moment Type randomly.

The Moment Type should help the next prompt understand how to turn the candidate into a Shorts script.

# CANDIDATE DIVERSITY RULE

When possible, the 3–5 candidates should not all be the same object from the same angle.

If the episode has multiple strong moments, select candidates with different visual anchors or different moment functions.

For example, prefer a balanced set such as:

* one Object Mystery
* one Hidden Trace
* one Spatial Threshold
* one Light-Texture Close-Up
* one Sound Motif or Fade-Out Atmosphere

If the episode is built around a single central object, it is acceptable to select multiple candidates around that same object, but they must have clearly different functions.

For example, if the episode centers on a folded hanji letter:

* Candidate 1 may focus on the letter as an Object Mystery.
* Candidate 2 may focus on lantern light over the paper as a Light-Texture Close-Up.
* Candidate 3 may focus on faint ink strokes as a Hidden Trace.
* Candidate 4 may focus on wet courtyard / dry maru as a Spatial Threshold.
* Candidate 5 may focus on rain or fading light as a Sound Motif or Fade-Out Atmosphere.

If the episode centers on a shell:

* Candidate 1 may focus on the shell as an Object Mystery.
* Candidate 2 may focus on the tide line and dry rock as a Spatial Threshold.
* Candidate 3 may focus on moonlight on the shell as a Light-Texture Close-Up.
* Candidate 4 may focus on the pebbles and sea hum as a Sound Motif.
* Candidate 5 may focus on the shell remaining as the water grows distant as a Fade-Out Atmosphere.

If the episode centers on a pale blue light:

* Candidate 1 may focus on the light as a Soft Folklore Trace.
* Candidate 2 may focus on the blue glow touching a damp stone as a Light-Texture Close-Up.
* Candidate 3 may focus on mist thinning along the path as a Spatial Threshold.
* Candidate 4 may focus on pine wind or low footstep sound as a Sound Motif.
* Candidate 5 may focus on the blue trace thinning into shadow as a Fade-Out Atmosphere.

If candidates overlap, clearly mention the overlap in the Korean review section.

Do not force false diversity by inventing new objects or scenes.

Do not choose weak candidates only to create variety.

Quality is more important than artificial variety.

Does the moment create gentle curiosity without anxiety?

High score:

* object remains without explanation
* light appears without becoming scary
* trace is present but not solved
* placement feels unusual but calm
* sound continues without a clear source
* threshold holds a quiet contrast

Low score:

* no mystery
* too much explanation
* too close to horror

## C. Channel Fit

Does the moment fit Moonlit Eastern Tales?

High score:

* old Korea / Eastern texture
* moonlit atmosphere
* calm sensory tone
* adult bedtime story compatibility
* curious but not anxious

Low score:

* horror
* fantasy spectacle
* generic self-help
* children’s tone
* cultural lecture

## D. Long-Form Conversion Potential

Would this moment make the right viewer want to hear the full episode?

High score:

* gives a taste of the full atmosphere
* leaves a soft unanswered image
* does not spoil the ending
* connects naturally to the episode premise
* attracts long-form sleep story viewers

Low score:

* feels complete by itself
* reveals too much
* attracts the wrong audience

Can this moment become a 20–40 second Shorts script?

High score:

* one object
* one setting
* one sensory contrast
* simple emotional direction
* no complex background

Low score:

* requires backstory
* requires multiple scenes
* requires explanation
* depends on long dialogue
